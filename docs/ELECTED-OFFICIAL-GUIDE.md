# Elected Official Dashboard Guide

Elected officials see aggregated, anonymized constituent feedback by district without accessing individual case details.

## What You Can See

You have read-only access to:

- **Weekly signal counts** (flags and applause by topic)
- **Response metrics** (average response time, SLA compliance)
- **Trend analysis** (which issues are trending up/down)
- **Your district only** (not other districts)

You CANNOT see:

- Constituent names or emails
- Case details or internal notes
- Staff member information
- Which constituents complained
- Detailed complaint descriptions

## Access

You'll receive login credentials from your city's IT department.

1. Go to `https://respond.transparentcity.co`
2. Click **Sign In**
3. Your role automatically shows district dashboard

Dashboard appears after login.

## Understanding the Data

### "Top Flagged" List

Shows which **topics** constituents are flagging most, NOT which specific cases.

Example interpretation:
- "Pothole on Main Street" flagged 8 times
- Means: 8 constituents mentioned this location
- May indicate: Serious problem needs urgent repair
- What you CAN do: Ask Public Works department about Main Street status

### Response Time Metrics

**"Avg Response Time: 5 hours"**
- Average time from case created to staff first response
- Does NOT include time to final resolution
- Shorter is better (shows city is responsive)

**"SLA Compliance: 96%"**
- Percentage of cases responded to within deadline
- 96% = 1 case out of 25+ missed deadline
- Higher is better (shows accountability)

### Trends

Arrows show if issues getting better or worse:

```
↑ Potholes (up from 4 to 8)
  → New reports coming in, may need prioritization

↓ Parking (down from 6 to 4)
  → Situation improving, enforcement working

→ Parks (stable at 5)
  → Consistent level of concerns
```

## Use Cases

### Prepare for Community Meeting

Before town hall or constituent meeting:

1. Check weekly dashboard
2. Note trending issues
3. Check applause (good news to share)
4. Compare to previous weeks
5. Identify items you can address

Example talking points:
- "We've received 26 constituent signals this week"
- "Main issue: 8 people reported potholes on Main Street"
- "Good news: Constituents praised our new bike lane project (4 applause signals)"

### Request Department Focus on Problem

If an issue is trending up:

1. See "Pothole on Main Street" flagged 8 times
2. Send to Public Works director: "I'm seeing 8 constituent reports about Main Street potholes this week. Can we prioritize?"
3. Works because you have data backing your request

### Highlight Successes

Check applause section:

- "Park renovation project" got 5 applause signals
- Share in newsletter: "Constituents love our park improvements!"
- Use as political win

### Track Your Performance

Compare your metrics to previous weeks:

```
Week of Jan 15-21:
  Response time: 5 hours
  SLA compliance: 96%

Week of Jan 8-14:
  Response time: 7 hours
  SLA compliance: 94%

Improvement: 2 hours faster, 2% better compliance
```

Share improvement with constituent communications.

## Common Questions

### Why can't I see constituent names?

Protects constituent privacy. You see the issues they care about, not their identities. This encourages honest feedback and protects vulnerable populations.

### Why can't I see my staff's responses?

Staff responses are between staff and constituents. Your role is to see trends and respond strategically, not micro-manage staff.

### Can I see cases from other districts?

No. You see only your district's data to keep districts' data separate and prevent cross-district conflicts of interest.

### How is my district defined?

Your IT department configured which cases belong to your district, usually based on:
- Geographic boundaries
- Assigned addresses
- Service areas
- City council districts

### Why no constituent satisfaction scores?

System focuses on official metrics (response time, SLA compliance) which are objective and fair. Satisfaction surveys can be unreliable.

## Tips and Tricks

### Use Trends to Tell Story

Don't just look at current numbers. Compare to previous weeks to show progress:

```
Bad: "We got 26 flags this week"
Good: "We got 26 flags this week, down from 34 last week - showing improvement"
```

### Combine with Constituent Feedback

Dashboard is aggregated data. Add context from personal constituent conversations:

```
Dashboard: 8 potholes reported
You hear: People concerned about safety
Result: Pitch to Public Works as safety issue, not routine maintenance
```

### Share Positive Feedback

Applause signals are often overlooked. Share with staff and constituents:

```
"Constituents are praising our park renovation - 5 positive signals!"
- Share with Parks Director
- Feature in newsletter
- Build political capital
```

### Track Response Time Improvements

If response time is slow, follow up with departments and show improvement:

```
Jan 1: 8 hours avg response
Jan 15: 6 hours avg response
Jan 29: 5 hours avg response

Tell departments: "Great progress! We're 40% faster than month ago"
```

## Privacy

Your access is:
- Read-only (can't change any data)
- Logged (IT can audit who viewed what)
- Limited to your district only
- Compliant with privacy regulations (GDPR, CCPA)

## Troubleshooting

### Can't log in

Contact IT: support@city.gov

Include:
- Your email
- Your district
- Any error messages

### Dashboard not loading

1. Try refreshing page (Ctrl+R or Cmd+R)
2. Try different browser (Chrome, Firefox, Safari)
3. Check internet connection
4. Contact IT if persists

### Numbers don't look right

Could be due to:
- Data from previous day (dashboard updates nightly)
- Different time zone (compare same period)
- Filter settings (check selected topics)

Click **Refresh** button to reload latest data.

### Can't download report

1. Check file size (may be large)
2. Try different format (PDF vs CSV)
3. Contact IT to email directly
